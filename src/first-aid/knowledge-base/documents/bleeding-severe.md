# Bleeding — Severe / Life-Threatening

**Source: American Red Cross First Aid Guidelines; Stop the Bleed Campaign (ACS/DHS); WHO Emergency Care**

## Applicable Situations
Spurting or pulsating blood, large wounds, amputations, bleeding that does not slow within 5 minutes.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately (local emergency number). Severe bleeding is life-threatening — do not delay.
2. Use gloves or a plastic bag as a barrier if available. If not, use extra layers of cloth to protect yourself.
3. Apply firm, direct pressure to the wound with the heel of your hand using the thickest cloth available. Press as hard as possible.
4. Maintain continuous pressure for a minimum of 10 minutes without lifting to check. Releasing pressure disrupts clotting.
5. If blood soaks through, add more material on top — do not remove the first layer.
6. For limb wounds only, if direct pressure fails and bleeding is life-threatening, apply a tourniquet 5–7 cm (2–3 inches) above the wound. Use a commercial tourniquet or improvised one (belt or strip of cloth at least 4 cm wide). Tighten until bleeding stops. Record the exact time applied.
7. Do not remove an object embedded in the wound. Apply pressure around the object with padding on either side.
8. Lay the person flat. Elevate legs 20–30 cm if no spinal injury is suspected (shock position).
9. Keep the person warm using a blanket or clothing to prevent hypothermia.
10. Monitor consciousness and breathing continuously until emergency services arrive.

## Signs of Hemorrhagic Shock (Act Immediately)
Pale, cold, clammy skin; rapid weak pulse; confusion or loss of consciousness; rapid shallow breathing; extreme thirst.

## Do Not
- Remove a tourniquet once applied
- Give food or water (may need surgery)
- Leave the person alone
