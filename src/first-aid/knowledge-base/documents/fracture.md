# Fracture (Broken Bone) First Aid

## Overview
A fracture is a break in a bone. Improper handling can cause further injury, including damage to nearby blood vessels and nerves. Immobilization is the key first-aid principle.

## Signs of Fracture
- Snap or grinding sound at time of injury
- Swelling, bruising, deformity at the injury site
- Severe pain that worsens with movement
- Inability to use or move the limb normally
- Bone visible through the skin (open/compound fracture — emergency)

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Call emergency services** for open fractures, spine/pelvis/femur fractures, loss of sensation, or any fracture with severe pain or deformity.

2. **Stop any bleeding.** Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth around (not directly on) bone protrusions. Do not push exposed bone back in.

3. **Immobilize the injury.** Do not attempt to realign or straighten the bone. Splint the limb in the position found.

4. **Apply a splint.** Use a rigid material (rolled newspaper, board, stick) padded with cloth. Extend the splint beyond the joints above and below the fracture site.

5. **Secure the splint.** Tie with bandages, strips of clothing, or belts above and below the fracture — not over it. Check that it's firm but not cutting off circulation.

6. **Check circulation.** After splinting, check pulse, warmth, and sensation below the injury. Cold, numb, or blue extremities indicate impaired circulation — emergency.

7. **Elevate the limb if possible.** Raise above heart level to reduce swelling, only if it does not cause pain or require moving the injured area excessively.

8. **Apply ice.** Wrap ice in cloth and apply to reduce swelling. Never place ice directly on skin.

9. **Treat for shock.** Keep the person lying down, warm, and calm. Elevate legs if no spinal injury suspected.

10. **Do not give food or drink.** Surgery requiring anesthesia may be necessary.

## Spine/Neck Fracture Precautions
- If spinal injury suspected (after fall, motor vehicle accident, diving), do not move the person
- Keep head and neck in the position found — stabilize manually
- Log roll only if person must be moved (requires multiple helpers)

## Open (Compound) Fracture
- Bone visible through skin or wound near fracture site
- Cover wound with sterile gauze — do not touch or push bone
- Emergency services immediately — high infection risk

## Femur (Thigh) Fracture
- Can cause life-threatening internal bleeding (up to 1–2 liters of blood loss)
- Do not attempt to splint alone without training
- Emergency services immediately
