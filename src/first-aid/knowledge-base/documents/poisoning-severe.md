# Poisoning — Severe (Caustic, Drug Overdose, Carbon Monoxide, Loss of Consciousness)

**Source: American Red Cross; AAPCC; WHO Emergency Care; CDC**

## Applicable Situations
Unconsciousness, seizures, severe vomiting, difficulty breathing, severe abdominal pain, or known ingestion of a highly toxic substance (caustics, medications in large doses, industrial chemicals, carbon monoxide).

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately. Severe poisoning requires immediate hospital treatment.
2. If carbon monoxide poisoning suspected (headache, confusion, nausea in an enclosed space with gas appliances): evacuate everyone from the building immediately. Do not re-enter. Call emergency services from outside.
3. If the person is unconscious but breathing: place in the recovery position (on their side) to prevent aspiration if vomiting occurs.
4. If the person is not breathing: begin CPR. For suspected chemical exposure, use a face shield for rescue breaths or perform hands-only CPR.
5. Do not induce vomiting for: caustic/corrosive substances (acids, alkalis, bleach), petroleum products (fuel, turpentine), or if the person is drowsy or unconscious.
6. Do not give anything by mouth to an unconscious or semi-conscious person.
7. Bring the substance container to the hospital — this is critical for treatment decisions.
8. For drug overdose (opioid suspected — slow breathing, blue lips, unresponsive): if naloxone (Narcan) is available, administer per instructions. Call emergency services.
9. Decontaminate skin/eyes as above if chemical exposure occurred. Use gloves to protect yourself from contaminated clothing or skin.
10. Keep the person warm and still until help arrives.

## Substances That Must Never Prompt Vomiting
Caustic substances (bleach, drain cleaner, battery acid), petroleum products, button batteries, sharp objects, or any substance if the person is losing consciousness.
