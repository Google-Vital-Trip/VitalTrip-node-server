# Fall Injury — Minor (No Loss of Consciousness, No Suspected Fracture)

**Source: American Red Cross; Mayo Clinic; CDC Fall Prevention**

## Applicable Situations
Fall with minor cuts, bruises, or muscle soreness. Person did not lose consciousness, can recall the event, and can move all limbs normally.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Do not rush to help the person up — first assess for injury while they remain on the ground.
2. Ask the person: what happened, do they have pain anywhere, can they move their arms and legs?
3. Check for visible injuries: bleeding, deformity of limbs, swelling.
4. If no significant injury is found and the person is alert: help them rise slowly — roll onto their side, push to a sitting position, rest, then stand with support. Do not pull them up suddenly.
5. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth to bruised or swollen areas for 20 minutes. Elevate swollen limbs.
6. Clean any cuts with water and antiseptic, cover with a bandage.
7. Observe for at least 30 minutes. Ensure the person can stand and walk normally before leaving them alone.
8. Advise rest and OTC pain relief (ibuprofen for bruising and swelling; paracetamol if ibuprofen is contraindicated).

## When to Seek Medical Care
- Any head injury even without loss of consciousness — headache, nausea, or confusion after a head impact requires evaluation.
- Inability to bear weight on a leg
- Suspected fracture (localised bone pain, deformity, swelling)
- Wrist or hip pain after falling on an outstretched hand (very common fracture patterns)
