# Seizure — Severe / Status Epilepticus / First-Time Seizure

**Source: Epilepsy Foundation; American Red Cross; WHO Emergency Care; AHA**

## Applicable Situations
Seizure lasting over 5 minutes, seizures occurring back-to-back without recovery, first-ever seizure, seizure with injury, or seizure in water.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately if: seizure lasts over 5 minutes, person does not regain consciousness, second seizure begins, breathing is absent after convulsions stop, or this is the person's first known seizure.
2. Status epilepticus (continuous seizure over 5 minutes) is a neurological emergency with risk of brain damage and death. Do not wait and hope it stops.
3. Protect the person from injury: clear the area, cushion the head, do not restrain.
4. Do not put anything in the mouth. Do not attempt mouth-to-mouth during active convulsions.
5. If breathing does not resume within 30–60 seconds after convulsions stop, begin CPR immediately.
6. If available and trained, administer prescribed emergency medication (diazepam rectal, midazolam buccal/nasal) per the person's emergency action plan.
7. Turn the person onto their side (recovery position) as soon as the convulsions stop.
8. Monitor for repeated seizures and maintain side position until fully conscious.
9. Note start time, duration, and characteristics of the seizure to report to emergency services.

## Seizure in Water
Remove the person from water as soon as safely possible. Support their head out of water during the seizure. Call emergency services immediately — water inhalation and drowning are serious risks even in brief seizures.

## After the Seizure
Do not offer food, drink, or medications by mouth until the person is fully alert and can swallow safely.
