# Hypothermia — Severe (Core Temperature Below 32°C / 90°F)

**Source: American Red Cross; Wilderness Medical Society; WHO Emergency Care; AHA**

## Applicable Situations
Shivering has stopped (dangerous sign), severe confusion or loss of consciousness, rigid muscles, very slow or absent pulse, cardiac arrest. Core temperature below 32°C.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately. Severe hypothermia is life-threatening and requires hospital rewarming.
2. Handle the person extremely gently. Rough handling or sudden movement of a severely hypothermic person can trigger ventricular fibrillation (cardiac arrest).
3. Keep the person horizontal. Do not allow them to walk or stand — exertion can trigger cardiac arrest.
4. Remove from cold environment gently. Shield from wind and wet.
5. Remove wet clothing by cutting if necessary to minimise movement. Replace with dry insulation.
6. Insulate the person completely — wrap in blankets or sleeping bag including the head. Insulate from the ground.
7. Apply chemical heat packs or warm water bottles (wrapped in cloth) to the neck, armpits, and groin only — avoid direct skin contact.
8. Do not rub or massage the limbs. Do not put the person in a hot bath — rapid peripheral rewarming causes dangerous circulatory collapse.
9. If the person is unconscious: check for breathing and pulse for a full 60 seconds (pulse may be very slow and faint). If no pulse, begin CPR.
10. In hypothermia, a person is "not dead until warm and dead." Continue CPR until emergency services arrive and the person has been rewarmed — survival after cardiac arrest in hypothermia is possible with hospital rewarming.

## CPR in Hypothermia
Start CPR if no pulse is detected. Hypothermic cardiac arrest can be reversed with hospital rewarming. Do not stop CPR due to apparent death.
