# Burns — Minor (First-Degree / Small Second-Degree)

**Source: American Red Cross First Aid; Mayo Clinic; American Burn Association**

## Applicable Situations
First-degree burns (redness, no blisters); small second-degree burns smaller than 7 cm in diameter not on face, hands, feet, genitals, or over joints.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Remove the person from the heat source immediately. Do not put yourself at risk.
2. Cool the burn under cool (not cold or ice) running water for 10–20 minutes. This is the single most important step — start as soon as possible.
3. Remove jewellery, watches, or tight clothing near the burn before swelling begins. Do not remove clothing stuck to the burn.
4. Do not apply ice, ice water, butter, toothpaste, or any home remedies — these worsen tissue damage.
5. Cover loosely with a sterile non-stick gauze pad or clean plastic wrap to protect from infection and reduce pain from air contact.
6. Take an over-the-counter pain reliever (ibuprofen or paracetamol) if available and the person is not allergic.
7. Do not break blisters — intact blisters protect the wound from infection. If a blister breaks, clean gently and cover.
8. Keep the burned area elevated to reduce swelling.
9. Monitor daily for infection: increasing pain, redness spreading beyond the burn, pus, fever, or foul odour.

## When to Seek Medical Care
- Blisters larger than 2 cm or rapidly expanding
- Burns on face, hands, feet, genitals, or joints
- Signs of infection develop
- Pain not controlled with OTC medication
