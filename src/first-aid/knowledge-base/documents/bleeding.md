# Bleeding (Hemorrhage) First Aid

## Overview
Severe bleeding can be life-threatening if not controlled quickly. Prioritize stopping blood flow immediately.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Ensure scene safety.** Do not touch blood without gloves if available. Call emergency services (911 or local number) for severe bleeding.

2. **Apply direct pressure.** Press firmly on the wound using a clean cloth, gauze, or bandage. Maintain continuous pressure without lifting to check.

3. **Elevate the injured area.** Raise the bleeding limb above heart level to slow blood flow, unless a fracture is suspected.

4. **Do not remove embedded objects.** If an object is embedded in the wound, apply pressure around it — never pull it out.

5. **Add more material if soaked through.** If blood soaks through the cloth, add more material on top without removing the first layer.

6. **Apply a pressure bandage.** Wrap a bandage firmly over the cloth to maintain pressure. Do not wrap so tight it cuts off circulation entirely.

7. **Use a tourniquet for limb-threatening bleeding.** If direct pressure fails on an arm or leg, apply a tourniquet 5–7 cm above the wound. Note the time applied. This is a last resort.

8. **Monitor for shock.** Watch for pale, cold, clammy skin; rapid weak pulse; confusion; rapid shallow breathing. Lay the person flat and elevate legs 30 cm if no spinal injury suspected.

9. **Keep the person warm and calm.** Cover with a blanket to prevent hypothermia. Reassure them.

10. **Do not give food or drink.** The person may need surgery.

## Severity Assessment
- **Minor:** Small cuts, abrasions — clean and bandage
- **Moderate:** Deep lacerations — direct pressure, medical evaluation
- **Severe:** Spurting blood, large wounds, limb injuries — emergency services immediately

## Shock Prevention
Signs of shock: pale skin, confusion, rapid breathing, weak pulse. Treat by laying flat, elevating legs, keeping warm.

## Special Situations
- **Internal bleeding:** Suspect if person has been struck, has a rigid abdomen, or shows shock signs without visible wound. Emergency services immediately.
- **Nosebleed:** Pinch soft part of nose, lean slightly forward, breathe through mouth for 10–15 minutes.
- **Scalp wounds:** Bleed heavily due to blood supply; direct pressure usually effective.
