# Seizure First Aid

## Overview
A seizure is caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain. Most seizures last 1–3 minutes and stop on their own. The primary first-aid goal is protecting the person from injury during the seizure.

## Signs of a Seizure
- Sudden loss of awareness or consciousness
- Rhythmic jerking of arms and legs (tonic-clonic)
- Rigid, tense muscles
- Blank staring, unresponsiveness (absence seizure)
- Confused behavior or wandering (complex partial)
- Loss of bladder/bowel control
- Falling to the ground

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Stay calm and time the seizure.** Note when it started. Call emergency services if it lasts more than 5 minutes or if you don't know if the person has epilepsy.

2. **Protect from injury.** Clear hard, sharp objects away from the person. Cushion the head with something soft (folded jacket, your hands).

3. **Turn on their side.** Gently roll to recovery position (on side) once muscle rigidity allows, to keep airway clear and prevent choking on saliva.

4. **Do NOT restrain the person.** Never hold limbs down or try to stop the jerking movements — this can cause bone fractures or dislocations.

5. **Do NOT put anything in their mouth.** A person cannot swallow their tongue. Inserting objects risks broken teeth or a bitten finger.

6. **Loosen clothing.** Loosen tight collar or anything around the neck.

7. **Stay with the person throughout.** Do not leave until fully conscious and aware.

8. **After the seizure, assess responsiveness.** Most people are confused, tired, and disoriented (postictal state) for 5–30 minutes. This is normal.

9. **Speak calmly and reassure.** Tell them what happened. Do not offer water or food until fully alert.

10. **Do not offer water until fully conscious.** Risk of aspiration.

## When to Call Emergency Services
- Seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes
- Second seizure begins shortly after the first (status epilepticus)
- Person does not regain consciousness
- Person was injured during the seizure
- Seizure occurred in water
- Person is pregnant
- Person has diabetes
- First-ever seizure
- Unknown cause

## Recovery Position
Roll gently onto left side, bend upper knee for stability, tilt head back slightly to open airway. This prevents aspiration of saliva or vomit.

## Special Situations
- **Febrile seizure (child with fever):** Remove excess clothing to cool, do not restrain, call emergency if first seizure or lasts >5 minutes
- **Seizure in water:** Support head above water immediately, bring to shore
- **Diabetic seizure:** After recovery, offer sugar if person can swallow safely
