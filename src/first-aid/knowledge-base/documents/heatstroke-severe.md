# Heatstroke — Severe / Classic Heatstroke

**Source: American Red Cross; CDC; WHO Emergency Care; American College of Emergency Physicians**

## Applicable Situations
Core temperature above 40°C (104°F) with confusion, slurred speech, loss of consciousness, seizures, or hot dry skin. Life-threatening emergency.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately. Heatstroke is a life-threatening emergency — every minute of elevated temperature increases brain and organ damage.
2. Move the person to the coolest available environment immediately.
3. Begin rapid cooling without delay — this is the single most important intervention:
   - Immerse in cold water or ice water bath if available (most effective method)
   - If immersion not possible: apply ice packs or cold wet towels to neck, armpits, and groin simultaneously
   - Fan the person vigorously while misting with cool water (evaporative cooling)
   - Remove all clothing
4. Do not delay cooling to wait for emergency services — cooling must start immediately.
5. Do not give oral fluids if the person is confused or unconscious (aspiration risk).
6. If the person is unconscious and not breathing normally, begin CPR.
7. If available, place cold packs in both armpits, both groins, and around the neck — these areas have large blood vessels.
8. Continue cooling until the person becomes alert OR until emergency services take over.
9. Monitor for seizures — heatstroke can trigger them. Turn the person onto their side if a seizure occurs.
10. Do not give aspirin or paracetamol for heatstroke — they are ineffective and may cause harm.

## Cooling Target
Cool the core temperature to below 39°C (102°F) as rapidly as possible. In heatstroke, time to cooling determines survival and neurological outcome.
