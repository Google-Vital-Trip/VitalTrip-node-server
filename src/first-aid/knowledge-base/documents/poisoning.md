# Poisoning First Aid

## Overview
Poisoning can occur through ingestion, inhalation, skin absorption, or injection. Treatment varies significantly by the type and route of poison — always contact poison control or emergency services for guidance.

## Types of Poisoning

### Ingested (Swallowed)
- Medications, household chemicals, plants, alcohol, food poisoning

### Inhaled
- Carbon monoxide, smoke, gas leaks, chemical fumes

### Absorbed (Skin/Eyes)
- Pesticides, industrial chemicals, corrosives

### Injected
- Snake venom, insect stings, drug overdose

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Call emergency services or poison control immediately.** Do NOT wait for symptoms. Bring the container or substance if possible.

2. **If unconscious or not breathing, call emergency services and begin CPR** if trained.

3. **Do NOT induce vomiting unless specifically instructed by poison control.** Vomiting corrosives (bleach, acids, drain cleaner) causes additional burning of the esophagus. Vomiting hydrocarbons (petrol, turpentine) risks aspiration.

4. **For ingested poison (if conscious and alert):** Do not give anything by mouth unless instructed. Activated charcoal may be recommended by poison control — only if directed.

5. **For inhaled poison:** Move person to fresh air immediately. Stay low if gas is heavy. Ventilate the area. Do not enter without respiratory protection.

6. **For skin/eye contact:** Remove contaminated clothing. Flush skin with large amounts of clean water for 15–20 minutes. For eyes, flush with water continuously for 15–20 minutes, holding eyelid open.

7. **For snake/animal venom:** Keep the affected limb below heart level and immobilize. Remove jewelry near bite. Do not cut, suck, or apply tourniquet. Note the snake's appearance if safe. Emergency services immediately.

8. **Collect information for emergency responders:** Name of substance, amount ingested, time of exposure, age and weight of victim, symptoms.

9. **Do NOT give milk or water** unless instructed by poison control — may speed absorption of some poisons.

10. **Monitor breathing and consciousness.** Recovery position if drowsy. CPR if breathing stops.

## Carbon Monoxide Poisoning
- Symptoms: headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion — often mistaken for flu
- No odor, no color — silent killer
- Evacuate building immediately, leave door open
- Emergency services — oxygen therapy required
- Do not re-enter building

## Alcohol Poisoning
- Signs: unresponsive, vomiting while unconscious, slow/irregular breathing, blue-tinged lips
- Recovery position to prevent choking on vomit
- Emergency services — do not leave alone
- Do not give coffee or food

## Drug Overdose
- Opioid overdose: pinpoint pupils, slow/absent breathing, unconscious — give naloxone (Narcan) if available, emergency services
- Stimulant overdose: rapid heart rate, high temperature, seizures — cool environment, emergency services
