# Burns — Severe (Large Second-Degree / Third-Degree / Chemical / Electrical)

**Source: American Red Cross; WHO Emergency Care; American Burn Association**

## Applicable Situations
Burns covering a large area, third-degree burns (white/brown/black charred skin, no pain due to nerve destruction), chemical or electrical burns, burns on face/airway.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately. Severe burns are a medical emergency.
2. Ensure scene safety. For electrical burns, do not touch the person until the power source is confirmed off. For chemical burns, wear gloves and avoid contact.
3. Stop the burning process: remove the person from the source. For chemical burns, brush off dry chemicals before water; for liquid chemicals, flood with large amounts of water for 20+ minutes.
4. Do not remove clothing that is stuck to burned skin — this causes further tissue damage. Remove only loose clothing and all jewellery.
5. Cool large burns with cool (not cold) running water or wet cloths for 20 minutes. Do not immerse a large burn in cold water — this causes dangerous hypothermia.
6. Cover the burn loosely with a clean, dry dressing or clean sheet. Do not use fluffy cotton wool directly on the burn.
7. Do not apply any creams, butter, oils, or home remedies.
8. Lay the person flat and keep them warm — severe burns cause rapid heat loss and shock.
9. If airway burn is suspected (singed nose hair, hoarse voice, burns around face/mouth), maintain an open airway and prepare for rapid deterioration.
10. Monitor for shock: pale skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion. Treat for shock while awaiting help.

## Inhalation Injury Warning
Smoke and hot gas inhalation is immediately life-threatening. Move to fresh air and prioritise airway monitoring over wound care.

## Do Not
- Apply ice or cold water to large burns
- Break blisters
- Give food or drink (surgery likely needed)
