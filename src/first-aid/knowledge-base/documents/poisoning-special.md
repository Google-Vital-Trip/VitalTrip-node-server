# Poisoning — Special Situations (Children, Alcohol, Plant/Mushroom, Medication Overdose)

**Source: American Academy of Pediatrics; AAPCC; WHO; Mayo Clinic**

## Children — Household Product Ingestion
- Children are the most common poisoning victims; most cases involve household medications, cleaning products, and plants.
- Even small amounts of adult medications (iron supplements, blood pressure drugs, sleeping pills, diabetes medications) can be fatal in small children.
- Call Poison Control immediately for any child suspected of ingesting any medication or chemical — even if symptom-free. Symptoms can be delayed hours.
- Do not induce vomiting without Poison Control guidance.
- Store all medications and chemicals in locked cabinets out of children's reach.

## Alcohol Poisoning
- Signs: confusion, vomiting, seizures, slow or irregular breathing, pale or blue-tinged skin, low body temperature, unconsciousness.
- Place in recovery position to prevent aspiration of vomit.
- Do not give coffee, cold showers, or let the person "sleep it off" without monitoring — alcohol poisoning can be fatal.
- If unconscious and not breathing: begin CPR, call emergency services.
- Blood alcohol continues to rise after a person stops drinking — symptoms can worsen even if no more alcohol is consumed.

## Poisonous Plants and Mushrooms
- Unknown mushroom or plant ingestion: preserve a sample or photograph for identification.
- Symptoms may be delayed 6–24 hours (especially Amanita mushrooms causing delayed liver failure).
- Even if symptom-free: call Poison Control immediately with identification.
- Do not induce vomiting unless specifically directed.

## Medication Overdose (Paracetamol/Acetaminophen)
- Extremely dangerous because symptoms may be absent or mild for 24–48 hours while severe liver damage occurs.
- Any suspected paracetamol overdose requires immediate hospital treatment — antidote (N-acetylcysteine) is most effective within 8 hours.
- Do not wait for symptoms to develop.
