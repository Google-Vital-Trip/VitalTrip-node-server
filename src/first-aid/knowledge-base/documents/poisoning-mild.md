# Poisoning — Mild / Ingested Non-Caustic Substance

**Source: American Red Cross; American Association of Poison Control Centers (AAPCC); Mayo Clinic**

## Applicable Situations
Mild symptoms (nausea, mild stomach upset, skin irritation) after ingesting a potentially toxic substance. Person is conscious and alert with no severe symptoms.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call your regional Poison Control Center immediately for guidance specific to the substance involved — do not manage poisoning without expert guidance. (US: 1-800-222-1222; have local number saved when travelling.)
2. Identify the substance: keep the container, label, or take a photo if possible. Note the substance name, quantity, concentration, and time of ingestion.
3. Do not induce vomiting unless specifically instructed to do so by Poison Control. For many substances (especially caustic or petroleum-based), vomiting causes additional damage.
4. If the person is conscious and Poison Control advises it: give small sips of water or milk to dilute the substance.
5. Do not give activated charcoal at home unless directed by medical professionals.
6. For skin contact: remove contaminated clothing and rinse the skin thoroughly with water for 15–20 minutes.
7. For eye contact: flush the eye with room-temperature water for 15–20 minutes, holding the eyelid open.
8. Keep the person calm and seated. Monitor for any symptom progression.
9. Do not give home remedies (salt water, milk, raw egg) without Poison Control guidance.

## Information to Have Ready for Poison Control
- Exact name of the substance (read from the label)
- Amount ingested (estimate if unsure)
- Time of ingestion
- Person's age, weight, and current symptoms
