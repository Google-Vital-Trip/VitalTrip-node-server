# Hypothermia — Mild (Core Temperature 32–35°C / 90–95°F)

**Source: American Red Cross; Wilderness Medical Society; Mayo Clinic**

## Applicable Situations
Shivering (still present — a good sign that the body is warming itself), cold skin, clumsiness, slurred speech, confusion, fatigue. Person is conscious and shivering vigorously.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Move the person out of cold, wind, and wet conditions to a warm, sheltered area. Protecting from the environment is the first priority.
2. Remove any wet clothing gently. Replace with dry clothing, blankets, or sleeping bags. Wet clothing continues to extract heat from the body.
3. Insulate from the ground — cold ground extracts heat rapidly. Place insulation (sleeping pad, extra clothing) underneath the person.
4. Cover the head — significant heat loss occurs through the head.
5. Apply warm (not hot) compresses to the neck, armpits, and groin only if available. These areas warm the blood efficiently.
6. If the person is alert and able to swallow safely, give warm sweet drinks (warm water, warm sports drink). Avoid alcohol and caffeine.
7. Do not rub or massage the extremities — this can drive cold blood from the limbs to the core and cause dangerous cardiac arrhythmias.
8. Encourage gentle movement if the person is alert enough — muscle activity generates heat.
9. Rewarm gradually. Monitor for improvement in mental state and cessation of vigorous shivering, which indicates warming.
10. Seek medical evaluation even after apparent recovery — afterdrop (continued core cooling after rewarming begins) can occur.

## Reassuring Signs of Mild Hypothermia
Active shivering is the body's defence mechanism — its presence indicates the thermoregulatory system is functioning.
