# Hypothermia First Aid

## Overview
Hypothermia occurs when core body temperature drops below 35°C (95°F). It is life-threatening and requires gradual rewarming — rapid rewarming can cause dangerous cardiac arrhythmias.

## Stages of Hypothermia

### Mild (32–35°C / 90–95°F)
- Shivering (body's natural rewarming — a good sign)
- Slurred speech, impaired coordination
- Confusion, poor judgment

### Moderate (28–32°C / 82–90°F)
- Shivering stops (dangerous sign)
- Severe confusion, drowsiness
- Stiff muscles, slow movements
- Slow, shallow breathing

### Severe (<28°C / <82°F)
- Loss of consciousness
- Very slow or absent pulse
- Dilated pupils
- Skin appears blue-grey
- May appear dead — "Not dead until warm and dead"

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Call emergency services** for moderate or severe hypothermia, or if the person is unconscious.

2. **Move to a warm, sheltered environment.** Get out of cold, wind, rain, or water immediately.

3. **Handle gently.** A hypothermic heart is extremely vulnerable to arrhythmia — rough movement can trigger ventricular fibrillation. Do not shake or jostle the person.

4. **Remove wet clothing.** Wet clothing accelerates heat loss. Cut clothing off if needed to avoid excessive movement.

5. **Insulate from the ground.** Place insulating material (sleeping bag, blankets, clothing) underneath the person. Ground conduction causes rapid heat loss.

6. **Cover and wrap.** Cover with blankets, sleeping bags, or clothing including the head (significant heat loss via head). Wrap completely.

7. **Apply heat to body core areas.** Warm compresses (not hot) or warm water bottles wrapped in cloth to neck, armpits, and groin — never directly to skin.

8. **Do NOT rub or massage extremities.** This sends cold blood from extremities to the core, potentially triggering cardiac arrest.

9. **Do NOT give alcohol.** Alcohol dilates blood vessels and increases heat loss.

10. **Warm sweet drinks if conscious and able to swallow.** Warm (not hot) non-alcoholic, non-caffeinated beverages for mild hypothermia only.

## Water Rescue Hypothermia
- Keep the person horizontal when removing from water (sudden upright position can cause blood pressure drop)
- Hydrostatic squeeze effect — water pressure assists circulation; removing from water can cause sudden drop

## CPR Note
- Check for pulse for 60 seconds before starting CPR (pulse may be very slow)
- Continue CPR until hospital if started — hypothermic patients have survived after prolonged CPR
- Rewarming must occur in hospital before declaring death

## Frostbite (Companion Condition)
- Frozen extremities: white/gray, hard, numb skin
- Do not rub or massage frostbitten tissue
- Rewarm only if no risk of refreezing (refreezing causes worse damage)
- Immerse in warm water (37–39°C) for 20–40 minutes
