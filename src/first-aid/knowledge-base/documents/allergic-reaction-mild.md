# Allergic Reaction — Mild (Localised)

**Source: American Red Cross; American Academy of Allergy, Asthma & Immunology (AAAAI); Mayo Clinic**

## Applicable Situations
Localised hives, itching, redness, minor skin swelling after contact with or ingestion of an allergen. No throat tightness, breathing difficulty, or systemic symptoms.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Identify and remove the allergen: stop eating the suspected food, move away from the plant or animal, remove jewellery or clothing in contact with the trigger.
2. For skin contact reactions: wash the affected area thoroughly with soap and water to remove any remaining allergen.
3. Apply a cool, damp cloth to itchy or swollen areas to reduce discomfort.
4. Give an oral antihistamine (e.g., diphenhydramine, cetirizine, loratadine) if available and the person has no contraindications. Follow package dosing.
5. Apply 1% hydrocortisone cream to itchy hives if available — do not apply to face, eyes, or broken skin.
6. Keep the person calm and seated in a comfortable position.
7. Monitor closely for 30–60 minutes for any sign of progression to a severe reaction.
8. Advise the person to see a doctor for allergy testing and to carry antihistamines in future.

## Warning Signs of Progression (Act Immediately if Any Appear)
- Throat tightness or hoarse voice
- Difficulty breathing or wheezing
- Swelling of tongue or lips
- Dizziness, fainting, or rapid heartbeat
- Nausea, vomiting, or severe abdominal cramps

If any warning signs appear, treat as severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis).
