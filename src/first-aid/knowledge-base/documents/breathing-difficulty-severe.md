# Breathing Difficulty — Severe (Respiratory Distress, Near-Respiratory Arrest)

**Source: American Red Cross; AHA; WHO Emergency Care; GINA**

## Applicable Situations
Person cannot speak in full sentences, is using accessory muscles (neck, shoulder) to breathe, shows blue-tinged lips or fingernails (cyanosis), or has severe asthma not responding to inhaler. Respiratory rate over 30 per minute.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately. Severe respiratory distress is life-threatening.
2. Keep the person sitting upright — do not lay them down. Upright position uses gravity to assist breathing.
3. If the person is unconscious and not breathing normally, begin CPR immediately.
4. For choking (complete airway obstruction — person cannot cough, speak, or breathe): perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) — stand behind, make a fist above the navel and below the ribs, grasp with other hand, thrust sharply inward and upward. Repeat until the object is expelled.
5. For severe asthma: continue rescue inhaler every 20 minutes if conscious. High-dose beta-agonist is the only first aid available — hospital care is essential.
6. Do not leave the person alone. Monitor continuously.
7. If breathing stops: begin rescue breathing if trained. Two rescue breaths, check for pulse, begin CPR if no pulse.
8. For suspected tension pneumothorax (chest trauma, unequal chest movement, sudden severe respiratory failure): call emergency services and keep the person upright on the affected side.
9. Keep the person as calm as possible — fight response increases oxygen demand.

## Choking — Infant Under 1 Year
Do not perform abdominal thrusts. Give 5 back blows between shoulder blades with heel of hand, then 5 chest thrusts (two fingers on lower half of breastbone). Alternate until object is cleared or infant becomes unconscious.
