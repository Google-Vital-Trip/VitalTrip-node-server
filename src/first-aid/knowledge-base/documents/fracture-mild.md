# Fracture — Suspected Minor (Closed, Stable)

**Source: American Red Cross First Aid; Mayo Clinic Orthopaedics**

## Applicable Situations
Suspected closed fracture with localised pain, swelling, and bruising but intact skin and no obvious deformity. Common in fingers, toes, wrist, ankle.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Immobilise the injured area immediately in the position found — do not attempt to straighten or realign.
2. Apply a splint if available: use a rigid object (rolled magazine, cardboard, wooden board) padded with cloth on the injured side. Extend the splint beyond the joints above and below the fracture.
3. Secure the splint with bandages, strips of cloth, or tape — firm but not so tight as to cut off circulation.
4. Check circulation every 15 minutes: fingertip/toe colour (should be pink), capillary refill (press nail bed — should return to pink within 2 seconds), sensation, and ability to move digits.
5. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth for 20 minutes on, 20 minutes off to reduce swelling. Never apply ice directly to skin.
6. Elevate the injured limb above heart level if possible to reduce swelling.
7. Take over-the-counter pain relief (ibuprofen reduces both pain and swelling) if available and not contraindicated.
8. Transport to medical care for X-ray — all suspected fractures require radiological confirmation.

## Do Not
- Attempt to straighten or "pop back" the bone
- Apply a splint so tightly that it restricts circulation
- Allow the person to put weight on a suspected leg or foot fracture
