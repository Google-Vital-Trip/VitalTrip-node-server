# Breathing Difficulty First Aid

## Overview
Difficulty breathing can result from many causes: asthma, choking, anaphylaxis, chest injury, drowning, or cardiac events. Establishing and maintaining a clear airway is the top priority.

## Signs of Breathing Emergency
- Visible effort to breathe (using neck/shoulder muscles)
- Noisy breathing: wheezing, stridor, gurgling
- Blue or gray lips/fingertips (cyanosis)
- Inability to speak full sentences
- Rapid shallow breathing or very slow breathing
- Clutching throat (universal choking sign)

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Call emergency services immediately** for severe breathing difficulty, cyanosis, loss of consciousness, or suspected choking.

2. **Check the airway.** If unconscious, tilt head back and lift chin to open airway. Look for visible obstruction and remove only if clearly visible — do not blind-finger sweep.

3. **Sit the person upright or semi-upright.** Upright position maximizes lung capacity. Never force a person with breathing difficulty to lie flat.

4. **Loosen anything tight around neck and chest.** Remove tie, scarf, or tight collar.

5. **For asthma attack:** Help the person use their rescue inhaler (bronchodilator). Have them sit upright. Lean forward with hands on knees (tripod position). If no inhaler or no improvement in 15 minutes, emergency services.

6. **For conscious choking adult:** Encourage forceful coughing first. If coughing fails, give 5 back blows (heel of hand between shoulder blades) followed by 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver). Alternate until cleared or unconscious.

7. **Heimlich maneuver:** Stand behind person, hands one fist above navel and below breastbone, pull sharply inward and upward.

8. **For unconscious choking:** Begin CPR. Each time you open airway to give breaths, look for object and remove if visible.

9. **Hyperventilation (panic breathing):** Reassure and coach slow, controlled breathing. Do not use paper bags — risks dangerous CO2 buildup. Focus on exhaling fully.

10. **Monitor breathing rate and consciousness continuously.** If breathing stops, begin rescue breathing or CPR immediately.

## Specific Conditions

### Asthma
- Trigger: allergens, exercise, cold air, infection, stress
- Positioning: upright, leaning forward
- Rescue inhaler: 4 puffs with spacer, repeat after 4 minutes if needed
- Severe: unable to speak, silent chest — emergency

### Anaphylaxis with Breathing Difficulty
- Epinephrine immediately if available
- Emergency services — airway can close completely within minutes

### Drowning
- Remove from water
- If not breathing: 5 rescue breaths then CPR
- Emergency services even if person appears recovered — secondary drowning risk

## Choking — Infant (<1 year)
- 5 back blows (face down on your arm)
- 5 chest thrusts (2 fingers on breastbone)
- Never use abdominal thrusts on infants

## Choking — Yourself
- Call emergency services if possible
- Self-Heimlich: make fist above navel, thrust inward/upward OR thrust upper abdomen against hard edge of chair/counter
