# Burns First Aid

## Overview
Burns are classified by depth (degree) and size. Correct immediate treatment prevents infection and reduces tissue damage.

## Burn Classification
- **First-degree (superficial):** Redness, pain, no blisters — affects epidermis only
- **Second-degree (partial thickness):** Blisters, intense pain, red/white mottled skin
- **Third-degree (full thickness):** White, brown, or black leathery skin; little to no pain (nerve destruction); requires emergency care

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. **Remove from heat source.** Stop the burning process immediately. Remove the person from fire, hot liquid, or electrical source.

2. **Call emergency services** for burns larger than 3 inches, third-degree burns, burns on face/hands/feet/genitals/major joints, chemical burns, or electrical burns.

3. **Cool the burn.** Run cool (not cold/ice) running water over the burn for 10–20 minutes. Do not use ice — it causes further tissue damage.

4. **Remove jewelry and clothing.** Gently remove rings, watches, and clothing near the burn unless stuck to skin. Jewelry retains heat and swelling may make removal impossible later.

5. **Do not pop blisters.** Intact blisters protect against infection. If a blister breaks on its own, clean gently and apply antibiotic ointment.

6. **Cover the burn.** Use a sterile non-stick bandage or clean cloth. Do not use fluffy cotton that can stick.

7. **Do not apply butter, toothpaste, or oil.** These trap heat and increase infection risk.

8. **Manage pain.** Over-the-counter pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen) can reduce pain.

9. **Prevent hypothermia.** Extensive burns cause rapid heat loss. Keep the person warm while cooling the burn.

10. **Monitor for smoke inhalation.** Hoarse voice, coughing, singed eyebrows/nasal hair, facial burns — all indicate airway burns. Emergency services immediately.

## Chemical Burns
1. Brush off dry chemicals first before using water
2. Flush with large amounts of water for 20+ minutes
3. Remove contaminated clothing while flushing
4. Do not neutralize acids with bases or vice versa

## Electrical Burns
1. Do NOT touch the person if they are still in contact with the electrical source
2. Turn off power source or use non-conductive material to move source
3. Even small entry wounds may have severe internal damage — always seek emergency care

## When to Seek Immediate Emergency Care
- Burns larger than the person's palm
- Burns on face, hands, feet, genitals, buttocks, or major joints
- Third-degree burns of any size
- Chemical or electrical burns
- Inhalation injury suspected
- Burn victim is very young, elderly, or has chronic illness
