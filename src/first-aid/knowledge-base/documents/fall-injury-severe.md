# Fall Injury — Severe (High-Energy, Loss of Consciousness, Suspected Spinal Injury)

**Source: American Red Cross; WHO Emergency Surgical Care; AHA; ATLS Guidelines**

## Applicable Situations
Fall from height, high-speed trauma, loss of consciousness (even briefly), neck or back pain after fall, weakness or tingling in limbs, or any fall with severe mechanism.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Call emergency services immediately. Do not move the person.
2. Assume spinal injury until proven otherwise in any high-energy fall, any fall with loss of consciousness, or any person complaining of neck or back pain after a fall.
3. Manual spinal stabilisation: if the person is conscious and moving their head, calmly instruct them to keep still. Gently place your hands on both sides of the head to discourage movement if the person cannot keep still.
4. Do not move the person unless there is an immediate threat to life (fire, water, unstable structure). Incorrect movement can convert a partial spinal injury into complete paralysis.
5. If the person is unconscious: first priority is airway and breathing. A modified jaw thrust (without tilting the head) can open the airway while protecting the cervical spine. If not breathing, begin CPR — an airway takes priority over spinal precautions.
6. Control any bleeding with direct pressure while maintaining spinal alignment.
7. Keep the person warm to prevent shock.
8. Monitor consciousness level every few minutes: does the person know who and where they are? Any deterioration is a red flag.
9. Do not give food or water — surgery may be required.

## Head Injury Red Flags (Immediate Emergency)
Loss of consciousness at any point; confusion or amnesia; repeated vomiting; severe headache worsening over time; unequal pupils; weakness on one side of the body; seizure; clear fluid from nose or ear.
