# Bleeding — Minor Wounds

**Source: American Red Cross First Aid Guidelines; Mayo Clinic**

## Applicable Situations
Small cuts, scrapes, shallow lacerations where bleeding slows within a few minutes.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before touching the wound to prevent infection.
2. Rinse the wound gently under clean running water for at least 1–2 minutes to remove dirt and debris.
3. Apply gentle direct pressure using a clean cloth or sterile gauze. Hold firmly for 3–5 minutes without lifting to check.
4. Once bleeding stops, clean around the wound edges with mild soap; avoid getting soap inside the wound.
5. Apply a thin layer of antibiotic ointment (e.g., bacitracin) if available to reduce infection risk.
6. Cover with an adhesive bandage or sterile gauze pad secured with medical tape.
7. Change the dressing daily or whenever it becomes wet or dirty.
8. Watch for infection signs over the next 24–48 hours: increasing redness, warmth, swelling, pus, or fever.
9. Keep the wound dry and elevated when possible to aid healing.

## When to Seek Medical Care
- Wound edges gape open (may need stitches)
- Last tetanus shot was more than 5 years ago
- Signs of infection develop
- Wound caused by rusty or dirty object

## Severity Marker
Minor bleeding: slows within 5 minutes of direct pressure, no arterial spurting.
