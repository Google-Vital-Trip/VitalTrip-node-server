# Seizure — Mild / Known Epilepsy (Brief, Self-Resolving)

**Source: Epilepsy Foundation; American Red Cross; Mayo Clinic**

## Applicable Situations
Known epileptic patient, seizure lasting under 5 minutes, person fully recovers consciousness, no injury sustained.

## Step-by-Step First Aid

1. Stay calm. Most seizures stop on their own within 1–3 minutes. Time the seizure from the start.
2. Clear the area of any hard or sharp objects that could cause injury during convulsions.
3. Gently guide the person to the floor if not already there. Place something soft (folded jacket) under their head.
4. Turn the person onto their side (recovery position) if possible — this prevents saliva or vomit from blocking the airway.
5. Do not restrain the person's movements — you cannot stop a seizure by holding the person still, and restraint can cause injury.
6. Do not put anything in the person's mouth. People cannot swallow their tongue during a seizure. Objects placed in the mouth cause dental damage and injury to helpers.
7. Loosen any tight clothing around the neck.
8. Stay with the person until they are fully conscious and oriented.
9. After the seizure, speak calmly and reassure them. Post-ictal confusion (disorientation lasting minutes to an hour) is normal.
10. Help them rest in a quiet place. Ask if they have taken their medication today.

## When Not to Call Emergency Services (Known Epileptic)
- Seizure lasted under 5 minutes
- One seizure only
- Person returns to normal consciousness
- No injury occurred
- Person has a known seizure disorder and this is a typical episode
